Gateway Elective

Engineering 121 - Fall 2019

For this course we used a variety of computer skills and machine tools to solve a series of five design challeneges provided to us thoughout the course of the class. Some of these challenges included designing a "Nuclear Warhead Disarmament System" and a Happy Meal Toy

For our first design challenge we worked on rapidly building a tower using a deck of cards and tape, to make them as tall as possible.

For our second design challenge we worked on disassembling a clock, and through this disassembly we learned how to create a bill of materials along with creating a structured assembly and exploded assembly of our clock.

For our third design challenge we worked on creating a nuclear warhead disarmament system, where we would have a balloon be popped within a glass chamber.

For our fourth design challenge we worked on designing a happy meal toy that was safe for children to use while also being appealing to parents of children.

For our fifth design challenge we worked on creating a Rube Goldberg Machine with multiple electronic componenets, arduino components and circuit boards, which would allow for a golf ball to travel throughout the course smoothly
